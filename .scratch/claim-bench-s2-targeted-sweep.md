## Priority: HIGH — likely material

### C1: Append-only event ledger

**ESAA: Event Sourcing for Autonomous Agents in LLM-Based Software Engineering**
- arXiv:2602.23193, 2026
- "Autonomous agents based on Large Language Models (LLMs) have evolved from reactive assistants to systems capable of planning, executing actions via tools, and iterating over environment observations. However, they remain vulnerable to structural limitations: lack of native state, context degradation..."
- **Why material:** directly proposes event sourcing as the solution to agent state+context problems. The claim_bench JSONL event ledger is a narrow-domain instance of what ESAA generalizes. Worth checking if ESAA has a library/framework that claim_bench should consume rather than rebuild.

**ESAA-Security: An Event-Sourced, Verifiable Architecture for Agent-Assisted Security Audits of AI-Generated Code**
- arXiv:2603.06365, 2026 (same author as ESAA)
- Companion paper showing ESAA applied to a specific domain (security audits)
- **Why material:** this is exactly the "core + domain adapter" pattern the claim_bench plan proposes. If ESAA+ESAA-Security already shipped the core, claim_bench's Phase A-D may be reinventing it.

**ElephantBroker: A Knowledge-Grounded Cognitive Runtime for Trustworthy AI Agents**
- arXiv:2603.25097, 2026
- "LLM based agents increasingly operate in high stakes, multi turn settings where factual grounding is critical, yet their memory systems typically rely on flat key value stores or plain vector retrieval with no mechanism to track the provenance or trustworthiness of stored knowledge"
- **Why material:** explicit critique of current agent-memory designs for lack of provenance/trust tracking. Could be the "state of the art" the claim_bench plan should benchmark against.

**From Fluent to Verifiable: Claim-Level Auditability for Deep Research Agents**
- arXiv:2602.13855, 2026
- "A deep research agent produces a fluent scientific report in minutes; a careful reader then tries to verify the main claims and discovers the real cost is not reading, but tracing: which sentence is supported by which passage, what was ignored, and where evidence conflicts."
- **Why material:** precisely the problem claim_bench solves for the author's deep-research agents. This is a MUST-READ — if it proposes a concrete architecture, claim_bench should either consume it or justify divergence. May have a competing schema.

**Provenance-driven nanopublications: representing source lineage and trust networks for multi-source assertions**
- Menotti/Marchesin/Silvello, Int J Digital Libraries 2025, DOI:10.1007/s00799-025-00431-x
- **Why material:** directly addresses multi-source trust networks + provenance lineage. Relevant to AuthorityClass + parent_event_ids design. The plan references nanopublications 1.0 in prior art; this 2025 extension may already have a formal trust network model the plan should adopt.

### C7: FIRE-Bench / atomic claim P/R/F1

**Rethinking Atomic Decomposition for LLM Judges: A Prompt-Controlled Study of Reference-Grounded QA Evaluation**
- arXiv:2603.28005, 2026
- "Atomic decomposition -- breaking a candidate answer into claims before verifying each against a reference -- is a widely adopted design for LLM-based reference-grounded judges. However, atomic prompts are typically richer and longer, making it unclear whether any advantage comes from decomposition or from prompt length/richness..."
- **Why material:** this paper questions whether atomic decomposition adds any value beyond prompt length confounds. Could invalidate the entire FIRE-Bench atomic-claim scoring approach. MUST READ to see whether their conclusion is "atomic hurts", "atomic is neutral", or "atomic helps once confounds are removed."

**Beyond Precision: Importance-Aware Recall for Factuality Evaluation in Long-Form LLM Generation**
- arXiv:2604.03141, 2026
- "Evaluating the factuality of long-form output generated by large language models (LLMs) remains challenging, particularly when responses are open-ended and contain many fine-grained factual statements. Existing evaluation methods primarily focus on precision: they decompose a response into atomic cl..."
- **Why material:** FIRE-Bench weights precision and recall equally (F1). This paper argues recall should be importance-weighted. If the claim_bench plan lifts FIRE-Bench's scoring unchanged, it inherits an outdated weighting.

**AtomEval: Atomic Evaluation of Adversarial Claims in Fact Verification**
- arXiv:2604.07967, 2026
- "Adversarial claim rewriting is widely used to test fact-checking systems, but standard metrics fail to capture truth-conditional consistency and often label semantically corrupted rewrites as successful. We introduce AtomEval, a validity-aware evaluation framework that decomposes claims into subject..."
- **Why material:** validity-aware decomposition — adds a check the FIRE-Bench atomic scorer may lack.

**Dissecting Atomic Facts: Visual Analytics for Improving Fact Annotations in Language Model Evaluation**
- arXiv:2509.01460, 2025 (borderline date — just before cutoff)
- "existing definitions of atomicity are underspecified, with empirical results showing high disagreement among annotators, both human and model-based, due to unresolved ambiguity..."
- **Why material:** documents that "atomic" is not well-defined and has high inter-annotator disagreement. Challenges the premise of FIRE-Bench.
