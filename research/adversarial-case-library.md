# Adversarial Case Library: The Best "Calling BS" Reviews

Curated 2026-04-04. The strongest adversarial reviews, meta-critiques, and field-level
demolitions — pieces where someone marshaled evidence to overturn a widely-held belief,
showed a celebrated result was noise, or proved an entire field was built on sand.

Selection criteria: (1) evidence quality, not rhetoric; (2) the target must have been
genuinely believed by serious people; (3) the critique must have teeth — changed practice,
forced retractions, or remain unanswered.

---

## Tier 1: Field-Level Kills

These didn't just critique a finding — they killed or mortally wounded an entire research program.

### The Candidate Gene Massacre
**Border et al. (2019)** "No Support for Historical Candidate Gene or Candidate Gene-by-Interaction Hypotheses for Major Depression Across Multiple Large Samples"
*American Journal of Psychiatry* | DOI: 10.1176/appi.ajp.2018.18070881

18 most-studied candidate genes for depression (5-HTTLPR, BDNF, COMT, etc.), each with 10+ publications, hundreds of papers total. Tested in 443,264 people. **Zero replicated.** Not weak effects — null effects. This paper is the death certificate for the candidate gene era in psychiatric genetics: thousands of papers, entire careers, tens of millions in funding, all noise. The strongest single demolition of a research program in recent memory.

*Also sourced in:* genomics/docs/research/adversarial_field_critique_2026-04-04.md

### The Ego Depletion Collapse
**Michael Inzlicht** — "The Collapse of Ego Depletion" (2025) + follow-ups
https://www.speakandregret.michaelinzlicht.com/p/the-collapse-of-ego-depletion

The researcher who *won the field's top prize* for ego depletion theory documents watching it fall apart. The multi-lab pre-registered replication found d = 0.04 (effectively zero). Inzlicht's follow-up ("Congratulations, You've Discovered Fatigue," Feb 2026) argues that what remains after stripping the failed theoretical apparatus is just ordinary tiredness — the "effect" adds nothing. His third piece ("Does Data Matter in Psychology?") escalates to a field-level indictment: psychologists routinely ignore data that contradicts their intuitions, raising the question of whether the field is genuinely empirical.

What makes this exceptional: a prize-winning insider recanting, not an outsider sniping.

### ENCODE's "80% Functional" Debacle
**Graur et al. (2013)** "On the Immortality of Television Sets"
*Genome Biology and Evolution* | DOI: 10.1093/gbe/evt028 | 493 citations

ENCODE's 2012 flagship papers claimed ~80% of the human genome is "functional," generating massive press coverage and reshaping funding priorities. Graur's rebuttal was devastating and witty — he showed ENCODE defined "function" as biochemical activity (transcription factor binding, histone modification), which is like defining a TV as functional because it emits electromagnetic radiation while showing static. Actual fraction of the genome under evolutionary constraint: **5-8.2%** (Rands et al. 2014). ENCODE's claim was not wrong in a subtle way — it was a category error dressed up in $288 million of data.

*Also sourced in:* genomics/docs/research/adversarial_noncoding_interpretation_2026-04-04.md

### Most Published Research Findings Are False
**Ioannidis (2005)** "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False"
*PLoS Medicine* | DOI: 10.1371/journal.pmed.0020124

Not an empirical finding but a mathematical proof: given realistic priors on effect sizes, researcher bias, and multiple testing, a positive result in most study designs is more likely false than true. Twenty years later, still the most-cited methodological paper in biomedicine. The radical move was treating "published finding" as a population and asking what fraction are true — no one had framed it that way before. Still unanswered at the structural level.

### Amyloid Hypothesis: Fabricated Foundations
**Charles Piller (2022)** "Blots on a Field?"
*Science* | https://www.science.org/content/article/potential-fabrication-research-images-threatens-key-theory-alzheimers-disease

Science Magazine investigation revealing image fabrication in the seminal 2006 *Nature* paper identifying Abeta*56 as a key Alzheimer's toxin. That paper anchored billions in amyloid-targeting drug development. Not a failed replication — forensic evidence of data manipulation in the foundational paper of a multi-billion-dollar research program. The amyloid hypothesis has other support, but its strongest "smoking gun" appears to have been fabricated.

## Tier 2: Celebrated Results That Were Noise

Individual findings widely taught, cited, and used for policy — that turned out to be artifacts.

### Hungry Judges
**Lakens** — "Impossibly Hungry Judges"
https://www.gwern.net/doc/www/daniellakens.blogspot.com/84802fb9cc19119f90c82476e3433684ba219f36.html

The Danziger et al. study claiming Israeli parole decisions track meal breaks (favorable rulings drop from 65% to near 0% before lunch, then reset) — cited in Kahneman's *Thinking Fast and Slow*, countless pop-science books, and policy discussions. Lakens showed the pattern is fully explained by case scheduling order (easy cases heard first in each session). The original authors missed this confound. A clean kill: the data pattern is real but the causal story is wrong.

### Dunning-Kruger Is a Statistical Artifact
**Alex Schumacher** — "The Real Dunning-Kruger Fools"
https://alexschumacher.com/real-dunning-kruger-fools/

The Dunning-Kruger effect — incompetent people overestimate their ability — is one of the most-cited results in all of psychology. Schumacher demonstrates it emerges automatically from any bounded scale with random noise: plot self-assessment vs. actual performance, and regression to the mean + autocorrelation produces the "effect" in datasets where self-assessment is literally random. The finding may be a statistical artifact, not a cognitive bias.

### The Hawthorne Effect Barely Existed
**Levitt & List (2011)** "Was There Really a Hawthorne Effect at the Hawthorne Plant?"
*American Economic Journal: Applied Economics* | https://gwern.net/doc/economics/2011-levitt.pdf

Went back to the original 1920s illumination experiment data and found minimal evidence for the "Hawthorne effect" — arguably the most-cited concept in organizational behavior and social science methodology. The effect that launched thousands of textbook chapters, informed every IRB protocol, and became a standard caveat in every observational study may have been noise in the original data.

### Reinhart-Rogoff: The Spreadsheet Error That Drove Austerity
**Herndon, Ash, Pollin (2014)** "Does High Public Debt Consistently Stifle Economic Growth?"
https://darribas.org/WooWii/Assignments/OriginalPaper.pdf

A graduate student found an Excel error (accidentally excluded 5 countries from an average) that invalidated Reinhart & Rogoff's claim of a sharp growth cliff at 90% debt-to-GDP. That claim directly influenced austerity policy in the EU, UK, and US during the worst economic crisis since the Depression. The canonical example of a single replication destroying a policy-driving result.

### Moretti's Missing Sort Command
**Chavez (2026)** "The Missing Sort Command"
https://carloschavezp29.substack.com/p/the-missing-sort-command

A coding error (missing sort command) in Moretti (2021) — one of the most-cited recent papers on agglomeration and innovation, with direct implications for billions in urban policy subsidies — invalidated the causal results. Accepted at AER in early 2026. A fresh Reinhart-Rogoff-style reversal. Shows the problem isn't historical — it's ongoing.

### Rosenhan Was Fraud
**Andrew Scull (2023)** "Rosenhan Revisited: Successful Scientific Fraud"
*History of Psychiatry* | https://gwern.net/doc/psychiatry/schizophrenia/rosenhan/2023-scull.pdf

Rosenhan's 1973 "On Being Sane in Insane Places" — where pseudopatients faked hearing voices to get admitted to psychiatric hospitals, then acted normally but weren't released — helped reshape American psychiatry, drove the DSM-III revision, and became one of the most-cited papers in all of social science. Scull's archival investigation found the study was fabricated: pseudopatients didn't exist as described, timelines don't match, and Rosenhan's own notes contradict the published account. Not a failed replication — proof of fraud in a paper that changed an entire medical profession.

## Tier 3: Field-Level Structural Critiques

Not single-finding kills but systematic arguments that an entire field's methodology is broken.

### The Replication Crisis Is Structural
**Gelman** — "Statistics and the Crisis of Scientific Replication"
https://stat.columbia.edu/~gelman/research/published/psych_crisis_minipaper.pdf

Documents how top psychology journals routinely publish "ridiculous, scientifically implausible claims" justified by p < 0.05. Names specific examples (ESP, menstrual cycle effects on voting). Gelman traces the structural incentives: researcher degrees of freedom, publication bias, and journals that reward novelty over rigor. From a working statistician — technically credible, not a polemicist.

### The Omnigenic Model: No Core Genes
**Boyle, Li, Pritchard (2017)** "An Expanded View of Complex Trait Architecture"
*Cell* | DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2017.05.038

If the omnigenic model is right — virtually every gene contributes to every complex trait via regulatory networks — then the entire enterprise of finding "the gene for X" is misconceived at the architectural level. Not a critique of bad methodology but a reconceptualization that makes the standard GWAS interpretation framework obsolete. There are no "core genes" for complex traits, only a regulatory web where every node matters a tiny bit.

### PRS Is Not a Clinical Screening Tool
**Hingorani & Wald (2023)** "Polygenic scores — are they ready for clinical use?"
*BMJ Medicine* | DOI: 10.1136/bmjmed-2023-000554

Meta-analysis of 926 PRS studies: median detection rate **11% at 5% false positive rate.** Median AUC 0.65. Number needed to genotype for CAD prevention: 5,882. The paper doesn't say PRS is scientifically wrong — it shows the clinical utility is too low for screening. The implication for DTC genomics: the entire "your genetic risk" framing oversells by an order of magnitude.

*Also sourced in:* genomics/docs/research/adversarial_field_critique_2026-04-04.md

### The Predictor Zoo: 15 Correlated Tools Masquerading as 15 Signals
**Livesey & Marsh (2025)** + genomics pipeline self-audit
DOI: 10.1186/s13059-025-03575-w

State-of-the-art variant effect predictors strongly correlate with each other but much less with actual experimental data (MAVE assays). Stacking 15 tools gives an "elaborate illusion of precision" — a confidence laundry where correlated uncertainties masquerade as independent validation. The 65-VEP benchmark (Radjasandirane et al. 2025) confirms diminishing returns beyond 3-5 tools.

*Sourced in:* genomics/docs/research/adversarial_predictor_zoo_2026-04-04.md

### Pathway Enrichment: Apophenia With Extra Steps
**Luo et al. (2009)** + **Wijesooriya et al. (2022)** + genomics pipeline self-audit

Random 100+ gene lists yield statistically significant pathway enrichment ~90% of the time. Only 4% of published enrichment analyses use proper background gene lists. Different pathway databases (KEGG, Reactome, WikiPathways) yield different "significant" pathways for the same gene list. No documented case of STRING/pathway analysis changing an individual clinical decision.

*Sourced in:* genomics/docs/research/adversarial_network_pathway_2026-04-04.md

### Behavioral Economics: The Fairy Tale
**Jason Collins** — "Bad Behavioural Science: Failures, Bias and Fairy Tales"
https://jasoncollins.blog/2016/05/11/bad-behavioural-science-failures-bias-and-fairy-tales/

Systematic takedown of the behavioral economics canon: priming, ego depletion, nudges. Traces how weak findings from small samples became the basis for pop-science bestsellers and policy recommendations. Targets the Kahneman/Thaler popular narrative specifically, naming which flagship findings have failed replication and which were always underpowered.

### US Dietary Guidelines: An Evidence-Free Zone
**Nissen (2016)** *Annals of Internal Medicine* | DOI: 10.7326/M16-0035
**Teicholz (2015)** *BMJ* | https://www.bmj.com/content/351/bmj.h4962

Nissen (Cleveland Clinic cardiologist, top-tier journal) argues the US dietary guidelines lack rigorous evidentiary support. Teicholz (BMJ) does the forensics: selective evidence review, dropped inconvenient RCTs, process capture by the Dietary Guidelines Advisory Committee. Together they show that nutritional guidance affecting 330 million people was set without the evidentiary standards applied to a single drug approval.

### Mammography Screening: 40 Years of Overselling
**Gotzsche** (Cochrane co-founder) + **Welch (2023)** *NEJM* DOI: 10.1056/NEJMp2307229

Gotzsche systematically dismantles the three claims used to promote mammography screening: that it saves lives, catches cancers early, and leads to less invasive surgery. Argues all three are empirically wrong and the screening establishment has consistently mislead women. Welch's NEJM dissent against updated screening guidelines provides the institutional counterweight. These are the most credible critics of screening orthodoxy — Gotzsche co-founded Cochrane and was expelled for this critique; Welch is the leading academic authority on overdiagnosis.

### The Placebo Effect Is Researcher Expectation
**birguslatro (Carcinisation)** — "A Case Against the Placebo Effect"
https://carcinisation.com/2024/11/13/a-case-against-the-placebo-effect/

Argues the placebo effect is largely an artifact of researcher expectation and sloppy controls, not a real healing mechanism. Placebo pills have almost no effect when administered by researchers indifferent to the placebo hypothesis, but enormous effects when administered by true believers. If correct, the "mind-body healing" interpretation of placebo is itself a placebo.

## Tier 4: Meta-Critique (How to Think About Critique)

### When Does Debunking Matter?
**Gwern** — "How Should We Critique Research?"
https://gwern.net/research-criticism

Meta-critique of research criticism itself. Most criticisms are cheap and uninformative because so many possible criticisms exist for any study. The valuable critique changes the probability of the conclusion enough to change your actions. A decision-theoretic framework for filtering signal from noise in the debunking literature. Essential reading before treating any critique (including the ones above) as decisive.

### Beware the Man of Many Studies
**Cremieux** — https://www.cremieux.xyz/p/beware-the-man-of-many-studies
**Ritchie** — https://stuartritchie.substack.com/p/meta-homeopathy

Inverts the usual "beware the man of one study" wisdom. In low-quality literatures, meta-analyses are *worse* than single well-designed studies because they aggregate garbage with precision. Ritchie demonstrates with a homeopathy meta-analysis. Together: the evidence hierarchy (systematic review > RCT > observational) breaks down when the underlying literature is contaminated.

### Medicine: The Only Five-Sigma Science?
**Ben Recht** — "Nothing's Shocking"
https://www.argmin.net/p/nothings-shocking

Argues that medicine is the *only* human-facing science that consistently finds five-sigma interventions, and challenges you to name another field that does. The implication: most social/behavioral science findings are in the noise and always were. From a Berkeley ML professor who brings quantitative rigor to what's usually hand-waving about effect sizes.

### How Life Sciences Actually Work
**Alexey Guzey** — https://guzey.com/how-life-sciences-actually-work

Year-long investigation into actual lab practice vs. public-facing norms. Documents specific mechanisms by which bad findings persist: unfunded replication, career incentives against null results, and the gap between what scientists say they do and what they actually do. Not a blog post — a sustained investigation.

## Tier 5: AI/ML Evaluation (Different Domain, Same Pattern)

### ML Peer Review Is Theater
**Desi Ivanova** — "The Illusion of Peer Review"
https://probapproxincorrect.substack.com/p/the-illusion-of-peer-review-part

Top-tier ML conferences (NeurIPS, ICML) have peer review that is inconsistent, poorly incentivized, and unable to catch basic methodological errors. From someone publishing in the field.

### Comparing the Uncomparable to Claim SOTA
**Benjamin Marie** — https://blog.benjaminmarie.com/2/comparing-uncomparable.html

Specific evaluation errors in GPT-3, PaLM, and AlexaTM papers — labs comparing results across incompatible experimental setups to claim state-of-the-art. Names the papers and the errors.

### Leaderboards Get Goodhart'd
**Ehud Reiter** — https://ehudreiter.com/2025/05/05/good-leaderboards-gamed/

Even well-designed leaderboards become targets and cease being good metrics. Specific NLP examples. The entire LLM evaluation paradigm is structurally broken, not just noisy.

### 70% of PRS Signal Is Confounding
**Scott Alexander (2025)** — Astral Codex Ten deep-dive on missing heritability
Synthesizing Tan et al. 2024, Sasha Gusev, Alex Young

Only 4 percentage points of 14pp PRS signal for educational attainment is direct genetic effect; 70% is confounding (assortative mating, genetic nurture, population stratification). The within-family analyses that separate direct from indirect genetic effects are the single most important methodological development in behavioral genetics — and they mostly show the headline numbers were wrong.

## Cross-Cutting Patterns

What makes these critiques strong (and what separates them from noise):

1. **Evidence, not rhetoric.** Every piece above marshals specific data, effect sizes, or methodological proofs. None relies on "this seems wrong" intuition.

2. **The target was genuinely believed.** Ego depletion won prizes. Rosenhan reshaped psychiatry. Reinhart-Rogoff drove austerity policy. Dietary guidelines affected 330M people. The bigger the prior, the more impressive the kill.

3. **Structural, not anecdotal.** The best critiques don't just find one bad study — they identify the mechanism that produced systematic error (publication bias, candidate gene methodology, enrichment analysis math, predictor correlation).

4. **Often from insiders.** Inzlicht on ego depletion, Gotzsche on mammography, Ioannidis as a working epidemiologist. Insider critiques carry more weight because the critic has skin in the game and domain knowledge to identify the real failure point.

5. **Unanswered.** The strongest test: has anyone successfully rebutted the critique? For most entries above, the answer is no — the fields moved on, quietly dropped the claims, or just ignored the evidence.

6. **Constructive null.** The best critiques don't just say "this is wrong" — they explain what the data actually show (scheduling order, not hunger; fatigue, not willpower depletion; coding error, not economic law). A specific alternative explanation is harder to dismiss than a vague objection.
